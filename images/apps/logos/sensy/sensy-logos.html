sensy — the mark & the five senses

The mark for sensy, the app that meets each moment through the body's five senses. One logo — the Pentad, five senses threaded to a single core — shown here at every size, followed by the five sense icons: Eye, Ear, Nose, Tongue and Hand. Pure SVG, holds down to 16 px, in sensy's language: neon mint lead, magenta where a sense comes alive.

The five senses — one mark each

The final sense icons: Eye (sight), Ear (hearing), Nose (smell), Tongue (taste) and Hand (touch) — 64×64, round strokes, mint lead with a magenta spark. These are the silhouettes sensy uses in-app.

How to read this. Up top is sensy's mark — the Pentad — shown from 128 px down to a 16 px favicon. Below are the five sense icons sensy uses in-app: Eye, Ear, Nose, Tongue, Hand. Marks lead with sensy's mint; magenta appears only where a sense fires — the iris at the eye's core, the taste spark.
