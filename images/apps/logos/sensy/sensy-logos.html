sensy — marks for the five senses

Candidate logos for sensy, the app that meets each moment through the body's five senses. Three marks for sensy itself, then three icon variants for every sense — sight, hearing, smell, taste and touch. Each mark is pure SVG, holds down to 16 px, and speaks sensy's language: neon mint lead, magenta where a sense comes alive.

The five senses — three takes each

One row per sense, three ways to draw it. Pick one variant per sense (or mix), and the winning silhouettes become sensy's in-app sense icons — 64×64, round strokes, mint lead with a magenta spark.

How to read this. The three cards up top are candidate app marks for sensy — each shown at logo scale, as a small wordmark lockup, and as a 16 / 24 / 44 px favicon proof. Below, every sense is drawn three ways so you can choose. Marks lead with sensy's mint; magenta appears only where a sense fires — the iris, the incoming wave, the taste-drop, the touch-ripple.
