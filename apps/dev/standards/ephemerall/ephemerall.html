About · ephemerality

Everything was passing anyway

Nothing here was built to last forever — and that is the point. From the first cave painting to the last unsaved draft, what we make leans toward disappearing.

For a century we tried to make the digital world permanent: every message archived, every photo backed up, every page cached against loss. Ephemerall asks the older question — what if forgetting were the default again?

It is a question culture keeps answering, in every era and medium:

The opposite impulse has its own monument: the Long Now Foundation is building a clock meant to tick for ten thousand years. Between the vanishing and the eternal sits a choice about what deserves to stay — which is exactly what the right to be forgotten and Ephemerall’s own Strategic Persistence are for.
