Fugy

One theme, set against itself — two voices chasing a single point.

A fugue is a subject that will not stay alone: it is answered, turned over, chased and crossed until the voices weave one thing out of many. Fugy takes that craft into Puzzy's Redefine family — a way to reshape meaning the way a fugue reshapes a line. Every mark below carries the same punctus, the note against which the other note is set.

The five devices

  • Subjectthe theme, alone
  • Inversionreflected in the axis
  • Canonthe round, chasing itself
  • Counterpointtwo voices trading register
  • Unfinishedwhere Contrapunctus XIV stops

The mark is the piece: two voices, one punctus, always a fifth apart.
