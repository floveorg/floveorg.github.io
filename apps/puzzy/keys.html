      About keys

      keys is a simple game for ordering ideas. Ten decks lie face down with 3 cards in each. Flip one and order its cards however you feel they should go, and (if you wish) record a short why behind that order.

      The Keys belong to you: the order you choose and the reason you give become the artifact.

      The more you tell your real views, the more you could learn about them with our tools at the end.

      💡 First vs second play — what changed under the new perspective?

      Click Insight to compare both plays — matches and differences light up in the tables below.

      💡 Tension — which decks were hardest to rank?

      The ones you hesitated on hold the most charge. A close call between two cards usually means both matter and the context decides.

      💡 Perspective — how did the bars shift things?

      Moving the perspective sliders re-weights the same cards. Where your second play diverged from the first is where the framing did real work.

      💡 Why — why this arrangement, for you?

      There is no right answer here. The order and the reason you give are the artifact — keep the version that feels most like yours.
