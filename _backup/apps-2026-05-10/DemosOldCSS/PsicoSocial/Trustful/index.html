Who & context

Two names, a relationship, and the situation this trust is meant for.

Scope of trust

Pick where you currently extend trust. Add a note per item if it helps. One scope at a time goes deeper than ten at once.

Why partial trust?

It's normal to trust someone highly in one area and more cautiously in another. People are reliable in some domains and conditions.

Trusty frames trust as scoped, time-bound and evidence-aware — realistic and fair. Precise scopes make trust more robust. Specific domains avoid blanket claims.

See other partial-trust declarations about other scopes in the links of the top right.

Confidence & time window

How much, and for how long. Both can grow with experience.

Why tell about relation and choose a timeframe?

Some people we barely know we'll trust forever and 100%, and the other way round.

Most trusts are only needed for a period of time, and some people can hold a higher degree of trust within a finite window. Trust can also be an experimental experience that triggers amazing future experiences — for one, the other, or both. Usually with less-known people we try short windows first, then extend as assurance accumulates.

Protection

State the boundaries that keep this trust healthy. Good limits are a gift for both sides.

Why getting so specific?

Good boundaries are a gift for both and all 🎁. They reduce ambiguity and prevent resentment. Write them as if both sides must win — don't feel paranoid about being more open. Bullies are not trustful people :)
