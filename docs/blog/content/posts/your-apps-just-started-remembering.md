Here's a small thing that quietly changes how flove feels to use.

Most apps forget you the second you close the tab — or, worse, they remember you
on *their* servers, forever. flove has been building the opposite: a place that's
**yours**, where the things you make in one app gather together, on your own
device, no account and nobody watching. That place is your **profile**. Today it
learned to fill itself.

## From a button you press to something that just happens

Until now, saving what you'd made to your profile was a **choice you had to
remember**: finish in an app, then press a button to send it over. That worked,
but it leaned on you to bother. As of today it's **automatic** — every time you
update an app's summary, that result lands in your
[profile](https://flove.org/apps/appy/appy-basic.html) on its own. No button to
find, nothing to remember. In flove's plan this is the step from *F0* (publish by
hand) to *F1* (publish always, quietly) — and it's live.

It's wired into seven apps so far — plan your
[last wishes](https://flove.org/apps/bio/pracsys.html), map who sees whom in
[goddy](https://flove.org/apps/metas/goddy.html), sketch a
[soul](https://flove.org/apps/metas/souls.html) — and whatever you make simply
shows up in your profile, ready when you come back.

And to be clear about the boring-but-important part: this is **local**. It saves
to *your* device, not to us. Nothing syncs anywhere, because there's nowhere for
it to sync to — that's the point. The apps you touch fill a notebook only you can
open.

## Until the next one

A tiny change, a nicer feeling: flove now remembers *for* you, without asking you
to remember to be remembered. Go make something in an
[app](https://flove.org/apps/), close it, wander off, come back — and find it
waiting. Built by one determined human and one alarmingly patient robot, still
going slow on purpose.

_slow it · flow it · love it._
